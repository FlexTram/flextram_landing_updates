The solution

One tram per crew.
One driver per tram.

FlexTram is a crew shuttle built for the realities of labor and staffing operations. A single compact tram moves up to 25 workers per trip from staging areas and parking lots to job sites — with one driver, on a predictable route your crew can count on.

Where you needed 5 vans and 5 drivers to move 100 workers to a job site, two FlexTrams and two drivers handle the same crew. Fewer vehicles on the road means lower insurance premiums, fewer accidents, and less maintenance overhead eating your margins.

FlexTram scales to your crew size — add trams for big jobs, return them when the contract ends. It navigates job site terrain that full-size vans can't access, and stores compactly between deployments. No idle fleet, no wasted capital.

Common questions

Can FlexTram replace passenger vans for crew transport?

Yes. One FlexTram carries up to 25 workers — replacing 2-3 passenger vans. Most staffing companies reduce their van fleet by 60-70% when switching to FlexTram, with corresponding drops in driver headcount and insurance costs.

How does FlexTram reduce liability for staffing companies?

Fewer vehicles means fewer insurance policies, fewer accidents, and less compliance overhead. A 3-tram fleet has a fraction of the liability exposure of an 8-van fleet moving the same number of workers.

Can FlexTram scale to different crew sizes and job sites?

Absolutely. Add trams for large jobs, return them when the contract ends. FlexTram's rental and lease options mean you only pay for the capacity you need — no oversized fleet sitting idle between jobs.

Can FlexTram navigate job site terrain?

Yes. FlexTram handles gravel, dirt, grass, and unpaved surfaces that full-size passenger vans can't access. It's built for the real-world conditions of construction sites, event grounds, and industrial facilities.

Is FlexTram cost-effective for temporary or seasonal staffing operations?

Yes. FlexTram offers short-term rentals, seasonal leases, and per-event options. You don't need to own a fleet — rent what you need, when you need it, and return it when the job is done.
