The solution

One tram. One driver.
Your entire campus connected.

FlexTram is a hospital campus shuttle designed for the unique demands of medical facilities. A single electric tram connects parking structures, outpatient clinics, emergency departments, and administrative buildings — carrying up to 25 passengers with one driver.

Where you needed 4 shuttle vans and 4 drivers, two FlexTrams handle the same routes with two drivers. Patients ride a predictable loop they can count on. Staff get from parking to their buildings faster. And the whole operation runs on electric — zero exhaust near patient care areas.

FlexTram is ADA accessible as standard — not a special configuration. Accessible boarding, appropriate aisle widths, and additional accessibility features are available on request. It's the hospital people mover built for patients first.

Common questions

Can FlexTram replace shuttle vans on a hospital campus?

Yes. One FlexTram carries up to 25 passengers — more than double a shuttle van — with one driver. Most hospital campuses replace 3-5 vans with 1-2 FlexTrams while improving patient satisfaction.

Is FlexTram ADA compliant for patient transport?

ADA accessibility is standard — not an add-on. Accessible boarding, appropriate aisle widths, and low-floor design make FlexTram ideal for patients with mobility challenges, wheelchair users, and elderly visitors.

Can FlexTram operate near patient care areas?

Yes. FlexTram is fully electric with zero exhaust — safe for covered drop-off areas, parking structures, and routes adjacent to patient care buildings.

How does FlexTram improve staff transportation on hospital campuses?

Staff spend less time walking from remote parking to their buildings. A predictable FlexTram loop gets nurses and doctors from parking to their posts faster — recovering clinical time that walking wastes.

What's the total cost savings vs. a shuttle van fleet?

Most hospital campuses see 50-60% lower total shuttle costs when switching to FlexTram. The savings come from fewer drivers, fewer vehicles, lower insurance, and reduced maintenance across the fleet.
