The problem

What limited mobility
is costing your community

Residents can't walk between buildings
Distance, weather, and physical limitations mean residents skip meals, miss activities, and stay isolated in their rooms instead of engaging with the community.
Golf carts aren't accessible
Standard golf carts can't accommodate wheelchairs, walkers, or residents with limited mobility. They serve the most able residents and exclude those who need help most.
Multiple drivers needed
Running 4-5 golf carts means staffing 4-5 drivers. In a labor market where caregivers are scarce, tying staff to transportation duties is a poor use of resources.
Weather exposure
Open golf carts offer no protection from rain, wind, or extreme heat — exactly the conditions most dangerous for elderly residents.

The solution

ADA-first transportation.
For every resident, every day.

FlexTram is an ADA-accessible senior community shuttle designed around the needs of elderly residents. A single compact tram connects dining halls, medical offices, activity centers, chapel, and gardens — carrying up to 27 residents with one driver on a continuous, predictable loop.

ADA accessibility isn't an afterthought — it's the foundation. Accessible boarding, appropriate aisle widths, and covered seating protect residents from weather and make every trip comfortable. Residents know exactly when and where the tram arrives.

FlexTram is quiet, compact, and low-speed — ideal for residential environments. It scales to any campus size, stores compactly, and frees up caregiving staff from driving duties so they can focus on what matters: caring for residents.

Common questions

Is FlexTram safe for elderly residents?

Yes. FlexTram operates at low speed on a predictable route. ADA-accessible boarding eliminates step-up hazards. Covered seating protects from weather. It's designed for the specific needs of senior residents.

Can FlexTram accommodate wheelchairs and walkers?

ADA accessibility is standard on every FlexTram. Accessible boarding, appropriate aisle widths, and configurations for wheelchair users and residents with walkers are included — not add-ons.

How does FlexTram improve resident quality of life?

Residents who couldn't walk between buildings can now attend meals, activities, medical appointments, and social events. Communities using FlexTram report measurable increases in meal attendance, activity participation, and overall resident satisfaction.

Is FlexTram quiet enough for a residential environment?

Yes. FlexTram is compact and agile with minimal operational noise — ideal for senior living campuses where peace and quiet are valued.

Can FlexTram scale as our community grows?

Absolutely. Add trams as new buildings open or census increases. FlexTram requires no permanent infrastructure — it adapts to your campus as it evolves.
