The problem

What sprawl is costing
your community experience

Amenities go unused
The pool is a mile from half the neighborhood. The fitness center is across the boulevard. Residents drive or skip it — and your community amenities are underutilized.
Too much car traffic
Short car trips between amenities clog community roads, fill parking lots, and create safety concerns around pools and playgrounds where kids are present.
Accessibility for all ages
Older residents, families with young children, and residents with mobility challenges can't comfortably walk or bike between amenities spread across the community.
HOA value proposition
Residents pay HOA fees for amenities they rarely use because getting there is inconvenient. The community's value proposition weakens when access is the bottleneck.

The solution

One quiet loop.
Every amenity connected.

FlexTram is a community people mover designed for master-planned neighborhoods. A single compact tram runs a predictable, quiet loop connecting pools, fitness centers, parks, commercial areas, and schools — carrying up to 27 residents with one driver.

Residents know exactly when and where to board. No car trips for a 3-minute drive. No parking hassles at the pool. Amenity utilization goes up, car traffic goes down, and the community becomes more walkable and connected.

The vehicle stays on your community grounds — same way the pool, the clubhouse, and the fitness center do. Run scheduled weekend loops to the pool and parks. Run evening commerce loops to retail and dining. Activate event mode for community gatherings, holiday programming, or developer model home days. Same vehicle, same driver, different patterns — your HOA owns the operating model.

FlexTram is ADA accessible as standard — serving residents of all ages and abilities. It operates quietly in residential areas, stores compactly, and scales as your community grows. HOAs and developers report measurable increases in resident satisfaction and amenity usage.

Common questions

Can FlexTram work as an HOA amenity shuttle?

Yes. FlexTram runs a fixed loop connecting all community amenities — pools, fitness centers, parks, commercial areas. Residents ride a predictable schedule instead of driving for short trips. HOAs report measurable increases in amenity usage.

Is FlexTram quiet enough for a residential neighborhood?

Yes. FlexTram operates quietly — designed for residential environments where noise matters. Residents won't hear it passing their homes, and it's far quieter than shuttle vans.

Can FlexTram serve residents of all ages and abilities?

ADA accessibility is standard. Older residents, families with strollers, wheelchair users, and children all ride comfortably. FlexTram makes your community accessible to everyone.

Can FlexTram scale as our community grows?

Absolutely. Start with one tram loop for your first phase, add more as new neighborhoods and amenities open. FlexTram scales to your community's build-out without permanent infrastructure.

Can developers use FlexTram for model home tours?

Yes. FlexTram is perfect for new home tours — shuttle prospective buyers between model homes, amenity centers, and sales offices. It showcases the community's connectivity and becomes a selling point itself.

How is FlexTram operated in a planned community?

FlexTram stays parked on community grounds — same way the pool, the clubhouse, and the fitness center do — and runs whatever pattern the HOA designs. Most communities run scheduled amenity loops during peak hours (pools and parks on weekends, fitness centers in early mornings, retail and dining on evenings) and shift to on-demand or event-specific patterns for community gatherings, holiday programming, or developer events. The HOA owns the operating model; the vehicle is part of the community asset list.
